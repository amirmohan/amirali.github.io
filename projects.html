CRISPRLand is a platform to analyze the machine learning algorithms trained on the samples from the fitness landscape of DNA repair after CAS9-induced breaks in the Walsh-Hadamard spectral domain. CRISPRLand provides an exponential speed-up in inferring the DNA repair landscape and redution in sample complexity (CRISPR experiments).

[C1] A. Aghazadeh, Orhan Ocal, and Kannan Ramchandran, CRISPRLand: Interpretable Large-Scale Inference of DNA Repair Landscape Based on a Spectral Approach, International Society for Computational Biology (ISMB), July 2020.

[C2] A. Aghazadeh, Orhan Ocal, and Kannan Ramchandran, CRISPRLand: Interpretable Large-Scale Inference of DNA Repair Landscape Based on a Spectral Approach, Bioinformatics, July 2020.

Code on Github

SPROUT is a machine learning algorithm based on random forests to predict the DNA repair outcome after CAS9-induced double-strand breaks in T Cells. Our analysis of DNA repair outcome using SPROUT reveals long insertions (> 20 nucleotides) from spacially close locations on the genome.

[C3] R. Leenay*, A. Aghazadeh*, J. Hiatt*, D. Tse, T. L. Roth, R. Apathy, E. Shifrut, J. F. Hulquist, N. Krogen, Z. Wu, G. Cirolia, H. Canaj, M. Leonetti, A. Marson, A. May, and J. MISSION is a first-order and BEAR is a second-order optimization algorithm for sublinear-memory feature selection in massively high-dimensional datasets. The key idea behind these algorithms is to store and iteratively update the model parameters in an efficient data structure from streaming called Count Sketch.

[S1] A. Aghazadeh*, R. Spring*, D. LeJeune, G. Dasarathy, A. Shrivastava and R. G. Baraniuk, MISSION: Ultra Large-Scale Feature Selection using Count Sketches, Proc. Intl. Conf. Machin. Learning (ICML), July 2018.
[S2] A. Aghazadeh, V. Gupta, A. Dewasee, O. Koyluoglu, and K. Ramchandran, BEAR: Sketching BFGS Algorithm for Feature Selection with Sublinear Memory, Submitted.

RHash is a fast algorithm to learn robust data-dependent hash functions. It minimizes the worst-case L-infinity norm distortion instead of the more conventional average ditortion to learn hash functions.

[H1] A. Aghazadeh, A. S. Lan, A. Shrivastava and R. G. Baraniuk, RHash: Robust Hashing via L∞-norm Distortion, Proc. Intl. Joint Conf. Artif. Intell. (IJCAI), Aug. 2017.

Universal Microbial Diagnostics (UMD) is a new sensing paradigm that we have develoepd at Rice University to detect microbial agents (e.g., superbugs and viruses) using a small number of target-agnostic randomized DNA probes. The inference algorithm solves a compressive sensing problem to identify the composition of the microbial samples.

[M1] A. Aghazadeh*, A. Y. Lin*, M. A. Sheikh*, R. A. Drezek, R. G. Baraniuk, et al., Universal microbial diagnostics using random DNA probes, Science Advances 2, e1600025 (2016).

Insense is an optimization algorithms to select optimal linear measurements to recover a (near-)sparse signal. We have shown the utility of Insense in designing DNA probes for the UMD platform.

[M2] A. Aghazadeh, M. Golbabaee, A. S. Lan, and R. G. Baraniuk, Insense: Incoherent sensor selection for sparse signals, Signal Processing 150, 57-65 (2018).
